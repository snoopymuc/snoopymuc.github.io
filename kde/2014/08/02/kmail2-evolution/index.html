Kmail2 Evolution

Semantic desktop and KMail2 - why, oh why?

I liked the kdepim/korganizer approach for its integration in the KDE Desktop, it's feature rich, configurable, integrated with all the KDE eye-candy. In my opinion: if Linux ever goes Desktop, then KDE would be my bet.

I enjoyed then in particular the features available with KMail2, be it the support for the ubiquitous use of HTML-eMails (creating eMails in claws-mail being the feature I really missed there), easily managing different identities, KDE-integrated support of PGP and S/MIME, and the really great eye-candy, up to support of "X-Faces".

So far, so good.

There's a feature coming with the PIM tools: the infamous semantic desktop nepomuk using the virtuoso backend, the dependency on either mysql or sqlite. First concerns have been about its performance - well, "640 kB ought to be enough for anybody." is past, my current PC copes well with KDE requirements.
Performancewise.
Maybe the preference of sqlite over mysql did good.

Accidentally, incidentally KMail2 - with kdm sessions staying open until kernel or KDE-upgrade - freezed from time to time: new eMails were available for getting read but not for getting deleted. Imap server is accessed in parallel by other MUA's, but this can not really be the cause.
Recipe for rectification: re-create the imap account. A nice occasion to consider if folders should be treated locally or server-side --- and that's the point where I made the worse experience of getting my home 100% filled by the nepomuk data at $HOME/.kde4/share/apps/nepomuk/repository/main/data/virtuosobackend and $HOME/.local/share/akonadi.

Okay, disk layout is flexible with lvextend and resize2fs, but then I was really baffled by the sizes.
Retrospecting the backups for the "normal" condition which I did not pay attention before:

  • IMAP storage: 1.4 G
  • $HOME/.kde4/share/apps/nepomuk/repository/main/data/virtuosobackend: 850 M
  • $HOME/.local/share/akonadi: 694 M

I'm kind of wondering why (, oh why?) it is "normal" to have the amount of meta-data necessary for the semantic backend surpass the amount of original data.

So, to bring that to an end I switched to Evolution (well, at least it does what I need), and after having removed the imap account in akonadi and having cleaned the backends the partition regained some room for further KDE "improvements":

  • $HOME/.kde4/share/apps/nepomuk/repository/main/data/virtuosobackend: 13 M
  • $HOME/.local/share/akonadi: 1.4 M

For indexing files, nepomuk allows to include/exclude directories. It might help to see a selective approach for mail storage as well?
